Keeping Fighters and Teams in Sync, Always

The UFC Athlete Companion App was designed as a single digital hub for fighters, coaches, and staff to manage logistics, compliance, and real-time coordination during fight weeks. From travel and lodging updates to instant location sharing, the app streamlined operations that were previously scattered across calls, emails, and manual check-ins.

Challenges

Fragmented Communication:

Fighters relied on scattered WhatsApp groups, emails, and manual
updates for schedules, logistics, and compliance.

High-Stakes Timelines:

A missed weigh-in, late medical check, or miscommunication could
derail events with global viewership.

Lack of Centralized Access:

There was no unified system for athletes and staff to access news,
schedules, and emergency contacts in one place.

Execution

Dynamic Info Section:

Designed a real-time hub with lodging, travel, weigh-in alerts, fight
schedules, and compliance checks all visible at a glance.

Visual Speed Dial:

Enabled one-touch communication with key team members, cutting
coordination lag during critical moments..

Service Index:

Centralized access to checklists, documents, accommodations, and
venue information

Introduction

The UFC Athlete Companion App was built to take the stress out of fight week. Instead of juggling papers, messages, and last-minute calls, fighters and staff could rely on one smart app. Every tap felt like a personal concierge—intuitive, informed, and always one step ahead.

The homepage itself was dynamic:
 A live notification banner shaped the layout, so the screen changed based on what mattered most in that moment.

Design Considerations

Notification-First UX:

Unlike static apps, this was built on the idea that “the homepage is alive.” The information architecture flowed from notifications outward, not from screens inward.

Context-Aware Prioritization:

A design matrix mapped each module’s notification style (Urgent, Contextual, Gentle, Collective). This ensured no overload, only relevance.

Scalability:

Microservice separation meant future modules (like nutrition, fan engagement, or training) could slot in without redesigning the core.

Fail-Safe Flows:

Offline sync, redundancy in API calls, and fallback states ensured fighters never faced a blank screen — even in low-connectivity zones.

Story Behind the Build

When we started, the challenge was simple to describe but hard to solve: How do you make an app that thinks like a concierge, not just a dashboard?

The answer was found in nodes, not screens.

Every module, Fight Schedule, Travel, Vault, Team Management, was built as its own intelligent node, each one capable of pushing data into a shared notification engine. Instead of working in silos, these nodes became active participants in a network.

The magic happened when we connected them together.

  • The Travel node didn’t just hold ticket data — it learned to notify the Schedule node when a gate change could affect weigh-in arrival.
  • The Vault node wasn’t just a folder — it nudged the To-Do node when a medical certificate was about to expire.
  • The Team node wasn’t just a roster — it triggered Communication broadcasts when a coach updated the training slot.

In other words, the app wasn’t a set of modules. It was a living system of conversations between nodes.

During development, our days and nights blurred into cycles of syncing and resyncing data. Time zones were no longer boundaries, New York QA teams, Indian dev teams, and Vegas fight-week testers were pushing updates at opposite ends of the clock. Each cycle produced a smarter notification model, tuned not just to display information but to decide what mattered most at that moment.

By the end of construction, the system could do something deceptively simple:

  • A fighter opened the app.
  • The homepage shifted instantly, showing only the most relevant banner.
  • Behind the scenes, five different nodes had already spoken to each other, traded context, and resolved what deserved the top spot.

That was the vision: not a tool that shouts every piece of data, but a concierge that whispers the right one.

Closing the Case Study

The UFC Athlete Companion App was never about building another utility. It was about reimagining fight-week logistics as a living system, one where every tap feels guided, every notification feels intentional, and every module works like a node in an intelligent network.

Even though the project paused due to organizational shifts, its design philosophy stands:
  • Notifications as architecture — the homepage as a dynamic surface, not a static grid.
  • Modules as nodes — powering each other, not competing for attention.
  • Context over clutter — always showing the right thing, never everything.

For me as a product designer, this project was a lesson in building not just screens, but experiences that think, adapt, and anticipate.

-Upamanyu Naskar
